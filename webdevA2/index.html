Saving Red Pandas

why it's important to conserve the red pandas

Biodiversity

Red pandas live in the eatern-parts of the Himalayas, which is home to more than 10,000 species of plants and animals, of which 163 species are globally threatened—asia's three largest herbivores (Asian Elephant, Greater One-horned Rhinoceros, and Wild Water Buffalo), and largest carnivore (Tigers).

Red pandas are one of its many ambassadors, who helps in bringing awareness to the fragile ecosystem known as the Himalayas before it's too late.

In conserving this ecosystem, it'll bring forth a more stable planet—off-setting the amount of carbon in the atmosphere, reducing the rate of which the Earth warms; and also may prove to provide essentail services for us.

Environmet Gage

During winters, temperatures may drop to -20℃. Red pandas tend to live at cooler altitudes—high altitudes, where it's cooler and more suitable for them; so as global temperatures rises, red pandas, will seek higher altitudes—cooler temperatures, meeting their needs. Thus, studying their behavior can allow us to gage on how the Himalayan ecosystem is fairing with climate change.

Conservation Efforts

Red Panda Network

They are committed in conserving the red pandas, and their habitat.

By educating and empowering the local communities that lives there—they expand the educational opportunities that the locals have, whom are often marginalized and/or have inadequate educational opportunities.

Fauna & Flora

A international wildlife conservation charity, which is set on protecting and restoring habitats; saving species from extinction, and developing livelihoods that are more sustainable—for those living closest to nature.

To conserve the Red Pandas, Fauna & Flora is supporting the creation of community-managed no-hunting zones—in the most critical areas of biodiversity; patroled by their highly trained rangers within that community—they look out for signs of illegal logging and poaching.

Do you know what kinds of food the red pandas eat? Let's find out, shall we.

Did you know that red pandas are carnivors?

Surprising? Well of course, the term "Panda" is offten associated with the giant panda, which is known for having a diet consisting of bamboo—a plant.

Red pandas eat bamboo too, y'know.

The reason why red pandas are able to eat bamboo, is due to evolution.

For bears, they hibernate during winter when food is scarce. However, "pandas" do not hibernate.

Why?

It's due to their low metabolic output—rate of which the body turns food into energy—during winter—when it gets very cold, they become dormant (temporary "stop" in growth, development and phisical activities).

It turns out that bamboo is the only avaliavle source of food for the "pandas" during winter.

That avaliability, lead to "pandas" convergently evoloving—orgainisms that evolve similar traits/featurs, while not being too closely related—around that same food source.

Fun Fact! The Red Panda and Giant Panda are only known to eat a few species of bamboo, one of which is Bashania Faberi.

Bamboo eaters, evolved to have relatively large skulls and molars—respective to their size; which allows efficient chewing, with disinct differences—distribution of mechnical stress (whilst chewing), and skull-stregth.

However unlike, other bamboo eaters, red pandas do not have any microbial bacterial that helps them processs the relativly nutrient spares bamboo—they thus adapted to pass all food out their gut at a rapid rate (2-4 hours) to meet their daily nutrient needs.

Thus, their ability to adapt to their surroundings, lead them to develop key features to help them survive during winter, and a diet that comprises mainly on the bamboo plant.
